  "% A self-contained statement of Keusch's solution to the 1-2-3 Conjecture\n\n\\begin{definition}[Graphs]\nA \\emph{graph} $G=(V,E)$ is finite and simple: $V$ is a finite set of vertices and\n$E\\subseteq \\binom{V}{2}$ is a set of unordered pairs called edges.\nFor $v\\in V$, the \\emph{neighborhood} of $v$ is $N_G(v):=\\{u\\in V:\\{u,v\\}\\in E\\}$ and the\n\\emph{degree} is $\\deg_G(v):=|N_G(v)|$.  Two vertices $u,v$ are \\emph{adjacent} if $\\{u,v\\}\\in E$.\n\\end{definition}\n\n\\begin{definition}[Isolated edge / $K_2$-component]\nAn \\emph{isolated edge} of $G$ is an edge whose two endpoints have degree~$1$ and\nform a connected component on exactly two vertices.\nEquivalently, a connected component isomorphic to $K_2$ (a single edge) is called a $K_2$-component.\n\\end{definition}\n\n\\begin{definition}[$k$-edge-weighting and weighted degree]\nFor an integer $k\\ge1$, a \\emph{$k$-edge-weighting} of $G$ is a function\n$\\omega:E\\to\\{1,2,\\dots,k\\}$.  The \\emph{weighted degree} (or \\emph{sum-color})\nof a vertex $v\\in V$ under $\\omega$ is\n\\[\n  s_\\omega(v)\\ :=\\ \\sum_{u\\in N_G(v)} \\omega(\\{u,v\\}).\n\\]\n\\end{definition}\n\n\\begin{definition}[Vertex-coloring edge-weighting by sums]\nA $k$-edge-weighting $\\omega$ is \\emph{vertex-coloring by sums} (or \\emph{neighbor-sum distinguishing})\nif for every edge $\\{u,v\\}\\in E$ we have $s_\\omega(u)\\neq s_\\omega(v)$.\n\\end{definition}\n\n\\begin{theorem}[Keusch, Solution to the 1--2--3 Conjecture, 2024]\n\\label{thm:123}\nLet $G=(V,E)$ be a finite simple graph with no $K_2$-component (i.e., with no isolated edge).\nThen there exists a $3$-edge-weighting $\\omega:E\\to\\{1,2,3\\}$ that is vertex-coloring by sums:\nfor every $\\{u,v\\}\\in E$,\n\\[\n   s_\\omega(u)\\ \\neq\\ s_\\omega(v).\n\\]\n\\end{theorem}\n\n\\noindent\nIn words: every graph without an isolated edge admits edge weights from $\\{1,2,3\\}$ so that adjacent\nvertices receive different sums of incident weights."
